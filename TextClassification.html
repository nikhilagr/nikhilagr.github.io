Predictive Model for Medical Text classification

Medical abstracts describe the current conditions of a patient. Doctors routinely scan dozens or hundreds of abstracts each day as they do their rounds in a hospital and must quickly pick up on the salient information pointing to the patientâ€™s malady. we developed design assistive technology that can identify, with high precision, the class of problems described in the abstract. In the given dataset, abstracts from 5 different conditions have been included: digestive system diseases, cardiovascular diseases, neoplasms, nervous system diseases, and general pathological conditions.

Methodology

  • Text preprocessing
    1. Text preprocessing include process of tokenizing, Stemming, Lemmatizing using Natural Language Processing Toolkit.
    2. Remove stopwords , punctuations.
    3. Implemented TF-IDF from scratch in python to assign weights to words.
    4. Represent text in sparse vectors.
  • Model Building:
    1. Build KNN Model from scratch and compared performance for different similarity measures such as cosine similarity, jaccard coefficient, euclidean distance.
    2. Results: Got F1 score of 78%

Challenges

  1. Dealt with highly imbalanced data, used various sampling techniques to deal with it.
  2. Selection of meta-parameter, determining value of K which prevents data from overfitting and underfitting of data.

Key Learning

Text Mining, NLP, Building KNN from Scratch.
